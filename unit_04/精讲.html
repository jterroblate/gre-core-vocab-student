sequester

/sɪˈkwestər/ · v. · Page 55

to take legal possession of property, or to keep someone or something apart from others

扣押；隔离；使隐退

联想

sequester 可以和 separate 联系：核心是“拿出来、隔开”。法律上可指扣押财产；一般语境中可指隔离、隐藏起来。

意思、用法与例句

依法扣押财产或资产

扣押；没收 用于法院、政府、军队等控制或扣押财产、资金、资产。

常见表达sequester propertysequester assetsproperty was sequestered

Rebel property was sequestered after the conflict ended.

查看翻译和讲解

冲突结束后，叛军财产被扣押。

sequester 表示强制占有或扣押财产。

把人或群体隔离开

隔离；使与外界分开 用于陪审团、证人、学者、群体被隔离以避免干扰。

常见表达sequester a jurybe sequestered from the publicsequester oneself

The judge decided to sequester the jury during the trial.

查看翻译和讲解

法官决定在审判期间隔离陪审团。

sequester a jury 是常见法律语境。

同义 / 反义

近义词

confiscate
没收
Customs officers confiscated the illegal goods. confiscate 是常见近义词，强调依法或强制没收。
seize
夺取；查封
Police seized documents from the office. seize 更宽泛，可指突然夺取或依法查封。
impound
扣押；暂扣
The city impounded cars parked illegally. impound 常用于车辆、动物或物品被官方暂扣。
isolate
隔离
The patient was isolated to prevent infection. isolate 接近 sequester 的隔离义。
seclude
使隐居；隔离
The writer secluded herself in a mountain village. seclude 更偏自愿或安静地远离人群。

反义词

release
释放；放出
The court released the documents to the public. release 与扣押或隔离相反。
return
归还
The authorities returned the property to its owner. return 与扣押财产相反。

词族 / 派生

重点记 sequestration / sequestered。

派生词

sequestration
隔离；扣押；封存
The sequestration of assets continued during the investigation.

同根 / 迁移联想

confiscate
没收
The state confiscated the smuggled weapons.

artificial

/ˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃəl/ · adj. · Page 55

made or arranged by people rather than occurring naturally; sometimes false or contrived

人为的；不自然的；虚假的

联想

artificial 和 natural 相对：它可以是“人造的”，也可以是“不自然、刻意造出来的”。

意思、用法与例句

由人制造或人为安排

人为的；人造的 用于材料、光线、智能、分类、边界等不是自然形成。

常见表达artificial lightartificial intelligencean artificial divisionartificial boundaries

The artificial division of people into age groups can hide important differences within each group.

查看翻译和讲解

把人们人为地按年龄分组，可能会掩盖每组内部的重要差异。

artificial division 表示人为划分。

说法、情境或行为显得不自然

虚假的；造作的 用于情节、礼貌、笑容、概念、争论等显得刻意、牵强。

常见表达an artificial smilean artificial distinctionartificial politeness

The critic argued that the conflict in the play felt artificial rather than psychologically real.

查看翻译和讲解

评论家认为剧中的冲突显得造作，而不是真有心理真实感。

artificial 可批评某物“不自然、刻意造出来”。

同义 / 反义

近义词

man-made
人造的
The lake is man-made, not natural. man-made 只强调人造；artificial 还可带“不自然、虚假”评价。
fake
假的
The museum removed a fake painting from the exhibit. fake 更直接表示假。
contrived
刻意的；不自然的
The final scene felt contrived and unconvincing. contrived 强调人为设计痕迹太重。
spurious
虚假的；伪造的
The argument rests on a spurious distinction. spurious 更正式，常指似是而非或不真实。

反义词

natural
自然的
The cave was formed by natural processes. natural 是最直接反义词。
genuine
真实的；真诚的
Her concern seemed genuine. genuine 与 artificial 的虚假或造作相反。

词族 / 派生

artificial 来自 art / artifice 的“人工制作”方向。不要把它只理解成科技词。

派生词

artifice
巧计；人为手段
The scene relied on artifice rather than honest emotion.
artificiality
人为性；不自然
The artificiality of the dialogue made the film hard to believe.
artificially
人为地；不自然地
Prices were kept artificially low.

同根 / 迁移联想

artifact
人工制品
The archaeologists found a stone artifact near the river.

patriotic

/ˌpeɪtriˈɑːtɪk/ · adj. · Page 55

showing love for and loyalty to one’s country

爱国的；有爱国心的

联想

patriotic 来自 patriot（爱国者）。它强调对国家的热爱和支持，但不必等同于盲目排外。

意思、用法与例句

表达对国家的热爱和支持

爱国的；有爱国心的 用于人物、演讲、歌曲、义务、行动表达对国家的忠诚和支持。

常见表达a patriotic citizena patriotic statesmanpatriotic dutypatriotic songs

The novel presents him as a patriotic statesman who sacrifices comfort for public service.

查看翻译和讲解

小说把他描绘成一位爱国政治家，为公共服务牺牲个人安逸。

patriotic statesman

For many soldiers, defending the city was a patriotic duty.

查看翻译和讲解

对许多士兵来说，保卫这座城市是一项爱国责任。

patriotic duty 表示出于爱国心的责任。

同义 / 反义

近义词

loyal
忠诚的
She remained loyal to her country during the crisis. loyal 更宽泛，可忠于人、组织、国家。
devoted
忠诚投入的
The doctor was devoted to serving rural communities. devoted 强调投入和忠诚，不一定是对国家。
nationalistic
民族主义的
The campaign used strongly nationalistic language. nationalistic 可带排外或强硬色彩；patriotic 通常更中性正面。
civic-minded
有公民意识的
Civic-minded residents volunteered to clean the park. civic-minded 偏公共责任，不一定是国家层面的爱国。

反义词

unpatriotic
不爱国的
Critics were unfairly labeled unpatriotic for questioning the war. unpatriotic 是直接反义词。
treasonous
叛国的
The general was accused of treasonous cooperation with the enemy. treasonous 语气极重，指叛国。

词族 / 派生

patri- 与“父国、祖国”有关。patriotic 是形容词，patriotism 是名词。

派生词

patriotism
爱国主义；爱国心
One major theme of the novel is patriotism.
unpatriotic
不爱国的
The accusation of being unpatriotic silenced many critics.

同根 / 迁移联想

nationalism
民族主义
Nationalism can overlap with patriotism, but it may be more political or exclusionary.

indebted

/ɪnˈdetɪd/ · adj. · Page 55

grateful because of help received; or owing money

感激的；受惠的；负债的

联想

indebted 和 debt（债）有关。可以是实际欠钱，也可以是“欠人情”、受惠而感激。

意思、用法与例句

因帮助或影响而感激某人

感激；受惠 用于学术、生活、工作中承认自己受益于某人或某群体。

常见表达be indebted to someone for ...deeply indebted toremain indebted to

We are indebted to scientists for many of the comforts of modern life.

查看翻译和讲解

现代生活的许多便利都得益于科学家，我们应心怀感激。

be indebted to ... for ...

The author is deeply indebted to earlier scholars who preserved the documents.

查看翻译和讲解

作者深深受惠于那些保存文献的早期学者。

学术写作中常用 indebted 表示致谢。

实际欠债或背负债务

负债的 用于个人、家庭、公司或政府欠钱。

常见表达heavily indebtedan indebted companyindebted households

Many farmers became heavily indebted after several years of drought.

查看翻译和讲解

连续几年干旱后，许多农民负债累累。

heavily indebted 表示债务负担很重。

同义 / 反义

近义词

grateful
感激的
I am grateful for your patience. grateful 普通；indebted 更像“欠了人情”。
obliged
感激的；有义务的
I am obliged to you for your help. obliged 可表示感激，也可表示有义务。
beholden
受惠于；欠人情的
The senator was beholden to wealthy donors. beholden 常带“欠人情、受制于”的意味。
obligated
有义务的
The company is obligated to report safety failures. obligated 偏法律或规则义务。
in debt
负债
The family was in debt for years. in debt 是普通表达；indebted 更正式，也可表示感激。

反义词

ungrateful
忘恩负义的
It would be ungrateful to ignore their help. ungrateful 与 indebted 的感激义相反。
solvent
有偿债能力的
The firm remained solvent despite the recession. solvent 与 indebted 的负债困境形成对照。

词族 / 派生

debt 中的 b 不发音。indebted 可以理解为“处在欠债/欠人情的状态”。

派生词

debtor
债务人
The debtor asked for more time to repay the loan.
indebtedness
负债；感激
The report measured household indebtedness.

同根 / 迁移联想

creditor
债权人
Creditors demanded repayment after the company failed.

resilient

/rɪˈzɪliənt/ · adj. · Page 55

able to recover quickly from difficulty, pressure, or damage

有恢复力的；适应力强的；有韧性的

联想

resilient 的画面是“受压后弹回来”。人、经济、生态系统、材料都可以 resilient。

意思、用法与例句

人、群体或经济经历困难后恢复

恢复力强；有韧性 用于个人、社区、经济、组织面对危机后恢复。

常见表达a resilient communitya resilient economyemotionally resilientremarkably resilient

The local economy proved remarkably resilient after the factory closed.

查看翻译和讲解

工厂关闭后，当地经济表现出惊人的恢复力。

resilient economy

Children can be resilient when adults give them steady support.

查看翻译和讲解

如果成年人给予稳定支持，孩子也能表现出很强的恢复力。

resilient 可用于人面对困难后的恢复。

材料或系统受压后仍能恢复

有弹性；耐冲击 用于材料、生态系统、基础设施在压力下保持功能。

常见表达resilient materialsa resilient systemresilient infrastructure

The engineers chose a resilient material that could bend without breaking.

查看翻译和讲解

工程师选择了一种能弯曲而不断裂的有韧性材料。

材料语境中 resilient 接近“有弹性、有韧性”。

同义 / 反义

近义词

tough
坚韧的
The plant is tough enough to survive dry summers. tough 普通，强调耐受；resilient 强调受挫后恢复。
adaptable
适应性强的
Adaptable workers learn new tools quickly. adaptable 强调能适应变化。
robust
强健的；稳健的
The system remained robust under heavy use. robust 强调强健稳定。
hardy
耐寒耐苦的
Hardy plants can survive poor soil. hardy 常用于动植物能耐受恶劣条件。

反义词

fragile
脆弱的
The fragile system collapsed under pressure. fragile 与 resilient 相反。
brittle
易碎的；脆的
Brittle materials crack instead of bending. brittle 与材料韧性相反，也可比喻制度脆弱。

词族 / 派生

resilient 的核心是“弹回、恢复”。重点记 resilience。

派生词

resilience
恢复力；韧性
Community resilience depends on trust and preparation.
resiliently
有韧性地；能恢复地
The team responded resiliently after the early failure.

同根 / 迁移联想

elastic
有弹性的
Elastic material returns to its original shape.

hallmark

/ˈhɔːlmɑːrk/ · n. · Page 55

a feature or quality that is typical of, and helps identify, a person, thing, period, or style

典型特征；标志

联想

hallmark 可以和 sign 联系，但它更像“识别标记”：看到这个特征，就知道它属于某人、某风格或某制度。

意思、用法与例句

某人、作品或制度最有代表性的特征

典型特征；标志

常见表达the hallmark of ...a hallmark of his novelsa hallmark of the policyone hallmark of ...

Pessimism is the hallmark of his major novels.

查看翻译和讲解

悲观主义是他几部主要小说的典型特征。

Balance-of-power politics became the hallmark of the new administration's foreign policy.

查看翻译和讲解

均势政治成为新政府外交政策的标志。

hallmark 表示可识别的政策特征。

同义 / 反义

近义词

sign
标志；迹象
A high fever can be a sign of infection. sign 更普通；hallmark 更强调典型、可识别特征。
feature
特点；特征
One feature of the design is its simplicity. feature 泛指特征。
characteristic
特征；特点
Patience is a characteristic of her teaching style. characteristic 与 hallmark 接近，但 hallmark 更像代表性标志。
trademark
标志性特征；商标
Dry humor became the writer's trademark. trademark 可比喻某人的招牌特色。
signature
标志性的特征
The chef's signature is the use of bitter herbs. signature 可指个人风格上的标志。

反义词

anomaly
异常；不符合常规之物
This warm winter was an anomaly, not a hallmark of the region. anomaly 与 hallmark 的典型性相反。

词族 / 派生

hallmark 原指贵金属上的质量印记，后来变成“典型标志”。重点记 the hallmark of。

派生词

trademark
商标；标志性特征
The actor's trademark was a quiet, ironic smile.

embellish

/ɪmˈbelɪʃ/ · v. · Page 55

to make something more attractive or dramatic by adding details, decoration, or extra elements

装饰；润色；添枝加叶

联想

embellish 的画面是“加东西让它更好看/更动听”。可以是装饰物，也可以是故事里添油加醋。

意思、用法与例句

给作品、演说或物体加装饰细节

装饰；润色 用于给演说、文章、衣服、建筑、表面添加细节，让它更吸引人。

常见表达embellish a speech with anecdotesembellish a roomembellish a design

She embellished the speech with amusing anecdotes.

查看翻译和讲解

她在演讲中加入了一些有趣轶事来润色。

embellish with anecdotes

讲故事时添油加醋

添枝加叶；夸饰 用于叙述、回忆、报道被加上夸张细节。

常见表达embellish a storyembellish an accountembellish the facts

Over time, he embellished the story until it barely resembled the truth.

查看翻译和讲解

久而久之，他把这个故事添油加醋到几乎不像事实。

embellish 可带负面，表示夸饰事实。

同义 / 反义

近义词

decorate
装饰
They decorated the hall with flowers. decorate 偏具体装饰；embellish 可具体也可抽象。
add detail
添加细节
The writer added detail to make the scene vivid. add detail 是基础理解。
adorn
装饰；点缀
The crown was adorned with small stones. adorn 更书面，偏美化装饰。
embroider
刺绣；添油加醋
He embroidered the tale with impossible adventures. embroider 可比喻夸大故事。
exaggerate
夸大
The witness exaggerated the danger. exaggerate 偏扩大事实，和 embellish 的负面义接近。

反义词

simplify
简化
The editor simplified the sentence. simplify 与添加细节相反。
strip down
精简；去掉装饰
The designer stripped down the logo to a single line. strip down 是去掉多余装饰。

词族 / 派生

em- + bell / belle 的历史方向和“美”有关。embellish 可以理解为“让它更漂亮或更有戏剧性”。

派生词

embellishment
装饰；润色；夸饰
The memoir contains several obvious embellishments.
embellished
装饰过的；润色过的
The jacket was embellished with silver thread.

同根 / 迁移联想

beautiful
美丽的
Beautiful details can make a plain object memorable.

gadfly

/ˈɡædflaɪ/ · n. · Page 56

an annoying critic who provokes others into action, often by persistent questioning or criticism

不断批评、刺激他人行动的人；讨人嫌的批评者

联想

gadfly 原本是叮咬牲畜的小飞虫；比喻义是“不断叮人、逼人行动的批评者”。

意思、用法与例句

通过持续批评来刺激权力或公众

批评刺头；搅动者 用于政治、社会、公共讨论中不断提出尖锐批评的人。

常见表达a political gadflya social gadflyan intellectual gadflya public gadfly

The journalist became a political gadfly, constantly exposing the mayor's broken promises.

查看翻译和讲解

这名记者成了政治上的刺头，不断揭露市长没有兑现的承诺。

political gadfly 强调持续批评。

Though many officials disliked him, the gadfly forced them to answer uncomfortable questions.

查看翻译和讲解

尽管许多官员不喜欢他，这个刺头迫使他们回答令人不舒服的问题。

gadfly 可有正面作用：逼人回应。

同义 / 反义

近义词

critic
批评者
The critic challenged the museum's narrow view of art. critic 中性；gadfly 强调不断刺激、惹人烦。
nuisance
讨厌的人或事
The constant noise became a nuisance. nuisance 只说讨厌；gadfly 通常还会促使别人行动。
provocateur
挑动者；煽动者
The activist was dismissed as a provocateur by his opponents. provocateur 更强，常有故意挑事意味。
agitator
鼓动者；煽动者
The government accused the speaker of being an agitator. agitator 强调鼓动群体行动，语气可负面。
reformer
改革者
The reformer proposed detailed changes to the law. reformer 可能推动改变，但不一定靠刺激批评。

反义词

loyalist
忠诚支持者
Party loyalists defended the leader despite the scandal. loyalist 与 gadfly 的持续批评姿态相反。

词族 / 派生

gadfly 是复合词：gadfly 原指叮咬牲畜的虫，学生可以用“不断叮人”的画面记住比喻义。

同根 / 迁移联想

provocateur
挑动者
The provocateur tried to make the crowd angry.
critic
批评者
A good critic can expose weak assumptions.

liberalization

/ˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ · n. · Page 56

the loosening or removal of restrictions, especially in an economic or political system

放宽管制；自由化

联想
