ministration

/ˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən/ · n. · Page 41

care, help, or service given to someone

照料；服务；帮助

联想

可以把它和 care、service 联系起来：ministration 更正式，强调对人的照料或服务。

意思、用法与例句

照料或服务某人

照料；服务；帮助

常见表达under someone's ministrationsnurse's ministrationsthe ministrations of a caregiver

He recovered quickly under the nurse's ministrations.

查看翻译和讲解

在护士的照料下，他很快恢复了健康。

under someone's ministrations 是比较正式的说法，强调受到某人的照料。

The shelter offered food, warmth, and the quiet ministrations of volunteers.

查看翻译和讲解

收容所提供食物、温暖，以及志愿者安静细致的照料。

这里 ministration 不只是“帮助”，还带有持续照顾的感觉。

同义 / 反义

近义词

care
照料
The patient needs careful care after surgery. care 最普通；ministration 更正式，常指实际提供的照料。
help
帮助
Her help made the recovery easier. help 范围很广；ministration 更强调照料性的服务。
succor
救助；援助
The refugees received succor from local charities. succor 更书面，常指困难中的救助。
service
服务
The organization provides free medical service in rural areas. service 可指制度化服务；ministration 更偏具体照料行为。

反义词

neglect
忽视；疏于照料
Neglect can slow a patient's recovery. neglect 与 ministration 的“照料”相反。

词族 / 派生

minister 原本有“服务、照料”的含义；ministration 可以看作这种服务行为的名词形式。

派生词

minister
牧师；部长；照料
The minister visited the sick after the service.
ministry
部门；牧师职务；服务工作
The ministry expanded its health program.

同根 / 迁移联想

administer
管理；施行；给予
The nurse administered the medicine carefully.

fall short

phr. · Page 41

to fail to reach a desired level, standard, or expectation

不足；未达到预期或标准

联想

short 是“不够长、不够多”；fall short 就是结果“差了一截”，没有达到要求。

意思、用法与例句

表现或结果没有达到预期

不足；未达到预期或标准

常见表达fall short of expectationsfall short of the standardfall short of the goal

The proposal has some good ideas, but it falls short of a workable plan.

查看翻译和讲解

这个提案有一些好想法，但还不足以成为一个可执行的计划。

fall short of 后面常接 expectation、standard、goal 这类目标或标准。

Compared with her earlier novel, this book falls short.

查看翻译和讲解

和她早期的小说相比，这本书逊色不少。

不一定要说出具体标准，也可以直接表示“没有之前好”。

同义 / 反义

近义词

fail
未能做到
The plan failed to solve the central problem. fail 更直接；fall short 更强调和某个标准之间的差距。
be inadequate
不充分；不够好
The evidence is inadequate to support the claim. inadequate 是状态描述；fall short 更像评价结果没有达到要求。
underperform
表现不佳
The new policy underperformed in its first year. underperform 常用于表现低于预期。
prove insufficient
证明不够充分
The initial data proved insufficient for a firm conclusion. 更正式，常用于证据、数据或解释不够。

反义词

meet
达到；满足
The study meets the basic standards of scientific evidence. meet a standard 与 fall short of a standard 相反。
exceed
超过
The results exceeded expectations. exceed 强调超出预期。

词族 / 派生

fall short 是固定短语，重点记 short 的“不够”意象，而不是拆词根。

派生词

shortfall
缺口；不足额
The city faces a budget shortfall this year.
shortcoming
缺点；不足
The report identifies several shortcomings in the design.

同根 / 迁移联想

insufficient
不足的
The evidence was insufficient to justify the decision.

hoax

/hoʊks/ · n.; v. · Page 41

a trick or false story made to deceive people

骗局；恶作剧；欺骗

联想

可以先和 trick 联系起来：hoax 是故意让别人信以为真的骗局，可能是恶作剧，也可能带恶意。

意思、用法与例句

制造假消息或骗局

骗局；恶作剧；欺骗

常见表达a bomb hoaxan internet hoaxexpose something as a hoaxperpetrate a hoax

There was no bomb in the hotel; the warning was just a hoax.

查看翻译和讲解

宾馆里根本没有炸弹；那条警告只是一场骗局。

bomb hoax 指虚假的炸弹威胁。

The photograph was later exposed as a hoax.

查看翻译和讲解

那张照片后来被揭穿是假的。

be exposed as a hoax 常用于说明某事被揭穿为骗局。

同义 / 反义

近义词

trick
诡计；恶作剧
The children played a harmless trick on their teacher. trick 可以很轻；hoax 更强调让人信以为真。
prank
恶作剧
The call was meant as a prank, not a real threat. prank 通常偏玩笑；hoax 可能造成恐慌或误导。
deception
欺骗
The deception misled thousands of readers. deception 是抽象的“欺骗”；hoax 是具体骗局。
fraud
欺诈
The scheme was prosecuted as fraud. fraud 更偏法律和金钱欺诈，语气更重。

反义词

truth
事实；真相
The evidence revealed the truth behind the rumor. truth 与 hoax 的“虚假骗局”相反。
genuine report
真实报道
The warning was a genuine report, not a hoax. 用于对比真实消息和骗局。

词族 / 派生

重点记它指“让别人信以为真的假事件”。

派生词

hoaxer
制造骗局的人
The hoaxer admitted that the story was invented.
hoaxing
制造骗局；欺骗行为
Online hoaxing can spread panic quickly.

同根 / 迁移联想

deceive
欺骗
The fake message was designed to deceive the public.

augment

/ɔːɡˈment/ · v. · Page 41

to make something larger, stronger, or more effective by adding to it

增加；扩大；增强

联想

increase 是普通的“增加”；augment 更强调通过补充让原有东西变得更多、更强或更有效。

意思、用法与例句

通过补充来增强资源或能力

增加；扩大；增强

常见表达augment resourcesaugment incomeaugment one's powerbe augmented bybe augmented with

Police units were augmented with newly recruited officers.

查看翻译和讲解

警察部队通过新招募的警员得到了扩充。

be augmented with 强调“用新增部分来扩充原有整体”。

The researchers augmented the survey data with interviews.

查看翻译和讲解

研究人员用访谈补充了问卷数据。

augment data 常用于学术语境，表示让资料更完整。

同义 / 反义

近义词

increase
增加
The program increased public awareness of the issue. increase 最普通；augment 更强调通过添加来增强。
add to
给……增加
The new evidence adds to our understanding of the problem. add to 更口语；augment 更正式。
enhance
提高；增强
Clear examples can enhance a student's understanding. enhance 强调质量或效果变好；augment 可强调数量或力量增加。
amplify
放大；增强
Social media amplified the impact of the speech. amplify 常有“放大影响或声音”的感觉。

反义词

reduce
减少
The policy reduced the cost of public transportation. reduce 与 augment 的“增加”相反。
diminish
削弱；减少
The scandal diminished his influence. diminish 更强调力量、重要性或程度下降。

词族 / 派生

augment 和 augmentation 都围绕“增加、扩充”这个核心。

派生词

augmentation
增加；扩充
The augmentation of staff improved the clinic's efficiency.
augmentative
有增强作用的
The device provides augmentative support for communication.

同根 / 迁移联想

supplement
补充
The course supplements lectures with online exercises.

interchangeable

/ˌɪntərˈtʃeɪndʒəbl/ · adj. · Page 41

able to be used in place of each other with little or no difference

可互换的；几乎没有区别的

联想

change 是“换”；interchangeable 就是两个东西可以彼此替换，意思或功能几乎不变。

意思、用法与例句

词语、零件或选择可以互相替代

可互换的；几乎没有区别的

常见表达interchangeable termsinterchangeable partsalmost interchangeableuse A and B interchangeably

In casual conversation, the two terms are often used interchangeably.

查看翻译和讲解

在日常谈话中，这两个词常常可以互换使用。

interchangeably 是副词形式，常和 use 搭配。

The machine was designed with interchangeable parts.

查看翻译和讲解

这台机器采用了可互换零件的设计。

interchangeable parts 指零件可替换，不一定完全相同。

同义 / 反义

近义词

equivalent
等同的；等值的
The two methods are roughly equivalent in accuracy. equivalent 强调价值或效果相等；interchangeable 强调可以替换使用。
exchangeable
可交换的
The tickets are exchangeable before the deadline. exchangeable 偏“可以交换”；interchangeable 偏“功能或意义可替换”。
substitutable
可替代的
In this recipe, butter is substitutable with vegetable oil. substitutable 更直接强调可替代。
fungible
可互换的；同质可替代的
Money is generally treated as fungible. fungible 常用于经济、法律或资源语境，强调单位之间没有个体差别。

反义词

distinct
不同的；有区别的
The two concepts are related but distinct. distinct 强调有明显差别。
incompatible
不兼容的
The two systems are incompatible without an adapter. incompatible 强调不能一起用或不能互换。

词族 / 派生

inter- 表示“相互、之间”；change 是“交换、改变”，合起来就是“彼此交换”。

同根 / 迁移联想

exchange
交换
The students exchanged notes after class.

congruent

/ˈkɑːŋɡruənt/ · adj. · Page 41

in agreement or harmony with something

一致的；相符的；和谐的

联想

可以和 consistent 联系起来：congruent 更强调两个目标、观点或形状彼此相符、没有冲突。

意思、用法与例句

观点、利益或目标彼此一致

一致的；相符的；和谐的

常见表达congruent withcongruent interestscongruent goalsvalues are congruent

Our interests are precisely congruent with yours.

查看翻译和讲解

我们的利益和你们的利益完全一致。

congruent 经常和 with 连用，表示两者相符。

The new evidence is congruent with the scientist's earlier hypothesis.

查看翻译和讲解

新的证据和这位科学家早先的假设相符。

学术语境中常用于说明证据和理论一致。

几何图形完全全等

形状和大小完全相同

常见表达congruent trianglescongruent figures

The two triangles are congruent, so their corresponding sides are equal.

查看翻译和讲解

这两个三角形全等，因此对应边相等。

这是数学中的严格用法，和日常“一致”义相关但更精确。

同义 / 反义

近义词

consistent
一致的；不矛盾的
Her story is consistent with the evidence. consistent 强调不矛盾；congruent 更强调相互吻合。
compatible
兼容的；相容的
The new software is compatible with older devices. compatible 常用于能共存或配合。
aligned
一致的；方向相同的
The policy is aligned with the university's mission. aligned 强调目标或方向一致。
harmonious
和谐的
The design creates a harmonious relationship between old and new materials. harmonious 带有协调、和谐的审美或关系色彩。

反义词

incongruous
不协调的；不相称的
The comic scene felt incongruous in such a serious film. incongruous 是直接反向词。
inconsistent
不一致的；矛盾的
His statement is inconsistent with the facts. inconsistent 强调和事实或前后说法冲突。

词族 / 派生

con- 有“一起”的方向；congruent 可以记成不同部分彼此合得上。

派生词

congruence
一致；全等
The study found congruence between public opinion and policy.
incongruent
不一致的；不协调的
His cheerful tone seemed incongruent with the bad news.

同根 / 迁移联想

congruity
协调；一致
The painting's colors have a pleasing congruity.

incongruous

strangely out of place or not in harmony with the surroundings

不协调的；不相称的；不合适的

联想

congruent 是“相符、协调”；incongruous 加了否定，就是某个东西放在环境里显得不搭。

意思、用法与例句

事物和场合或环境不搭

不协调的；不相称的；不合适的

Heavy walking shoes looked incongruous with her elegant evening dress.

查看翻译和讲解

厚重的徒步鞋配她优雅的晚礼服显得很不协调。

The joke seemed incongruous in a speech about national tragedy.

查看翻译和讲解

在一场谈论国家悲剧的演讲中，这个笑话显得很不合适。

常用于评论语气或内容和场合不匹配。

同义 / 反义

近义词

out of place
不合适；格格不入
The bright poster looked out of place in the formal office. out of place 更口语；incongruous 更书面。
inconsistent
不一致的
The conclusion is inconsistent with the data. inconsistent 强调逻辑或证据冲突；incongruous 更强调场景和风格不搭。
discordant
不和谐的；刺耳的
A discordant note interrupted the calm music. discordant 可用于声音，也可比喻意见或风格冲突。
anomalous
异常的；反常的
The anomalous result forced the team to repeat the experiment. anomalous 强调偏离规律；incongruous 强调不协调或不相称。

反义词

congruous
协调的；相称的
The simple furniture was congruous with the room's quiet style. congruous 是直接反义。
harmonious
和谐的
The colors create a harmonious effect. harmonious 强调整体协调。

词族 / 派生

in- 表示否定；congruous 表示协调、相称，所以 incongruous 是“不协调、不相称”。

派生词

incongruity
不协调；不相称
The critic noted the incongruity between the actor's costume and the scene.

同根 / 迁移联想

congruence
一致；协调
There was little congruence between the promise and the result.

这篇文章对法院判决作出了简洁明快的分析。

a crisp analysis 强调分析清楚、利落。

Her crisp reply ended the debate.

查看翻译和讲解

她干脆利落的回答结束了争论。

crisp reply 带有“简短而有力”的感觉。

食物脆或空气清爽

查看翻译和讲解

清爽的早晨空气让散步很舒服。

同义 / 反义

近义词

concise
简洁的

succinct
简明扼要的
The report gives a succinct account of the experiment. succinct 更书面，接近“简明扼要”。
terse
简短生硬的
His terse response suggested irritation. terse 可能带冷淡或生硬；crisp 通常更正面。

反义词

rambling
冗长散乱的
The essay was rambling and hard to follow. rambling 与 crisp 的“清楚利落”相反。
vague
模糊的
The instructions were vague and confusing. vague 强调不清楚。

词族 / 派生

共同意象是：边界清楚、质感利落。

派生词

crisply
干脆利落地
She answered crisply and returned to her work.
crispness
清晰利落；脆爽

lesser

smaller, less important, or lower in degree or status

较小的；较次要的；较低等的

联想

较小的；较次要的；较低等的

博物馆展出了这位艺术家几件较次要的作品。
