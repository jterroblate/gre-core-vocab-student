pledge

/pledʒ/ · v. · Page 275

to promise formally that something will happen or be true

正式承诺；保证

联想

promise 是普通的“答应”；pledge 更正式，常用于竞选、政府、组织或公开场合的承诺。

意思、用法与例句

公开或正式承诺

正式承诺做某事、支持某事或保证某个说法成立。

常见表达pledge to do somethingpledge that...pledge support / funds

During his campaign, he pledged that he would not raise taxes.

查看翻译和讲解

他在竞选期间承诺不会提高税收。

pledge that... 常用于政治或正式声明，强调公开承诺。

The organization pledged funds to rebuild the damaged library.

查看翻译和讲解

该组织承诺拨款重建受损的图书馆。

pledge funds/support 表示正式承诺提供资源或支持。

同义 / 反义

近义词

promise
承诺
The mayor promised to improve public transport. promise 最普通；pledge 更正式，常带公开责任。
commit
承诺投入；使承担义务
The company committed more resources to the project. commit 强调投入资源或承担长期责任。
vow
郑重发誓
The activists vowed to continue the campaign. vow 语气更强，像誓言；pledge 更偏正式承诺。
renounce
声明放弃
The party renounced violence after the election. renounce 是公开放弃某事，不是承诺去做某事。

反义词

renege
违背承诺
The leader was criticized for reneging on his campaign promise. renege on a promise 表示不兑现承诺，是 pledge 的反向动作。

词族 / 派生

派生词

pledged
已承诺的；被抵押的
The pledged funds arrived before the project began.

counterfeit

/ˈkaʊntərfɪt/ · adj./n./v. · Page 275

made to look real in order to deceive people

伪造的；仿造品；伪造

联想

fake 是最熟悉的“假的”；counterfeit 更强调“仿得像真的，并且目的是欺骗”。

意思、用法与例句

伪造物品或文件

仿造真实物品，让人误以为是真的。

常见表达counterfeit moneycounterfeit goodsa counterfeit document

The police seized thousands of counterfeit banknotes.

查看翻译和讲解

警方查获了数千张伪钞。

counterfeit banknotes/money 是最典型搭配，强调伪造并流通。

The necklace looked expensive, but the diamond was counterfeit.

查看翻译和讲解

这条项链看起来很贵，但钻石是假的。

这里 counterfeit 表示仿真品，重点是冒充真货。

虚假的情感或品质

也可形容表情、热情或道德姿态像真的，但并不真诚。

常见表达counterfeit enthusiasmcounterfeit sympathy

Her counterfeit sympathy only made the apology seem less sincere.

查看翻译和讲解

她假装出来的同情反而让道歉显得更不真诚。

正式写作中 counterfeit 可用于抽象品质，表示“装出来的”。

同义 / 反义

近义词

fake
假的；伪造的
The signature on the letter was fake. fake 最日常；counterfeit 更强调仿真和欺骗目的。
false
不真实的；错误的
The report was based on false assumptions. false 范围更广，可指不真实或错误；counterfeit 多指伪造物。
fraudulent
欺诈性的
The company was punished for fraudulent accounting. fraudulent 强调欺诈行为或非法目的。
spurious
假的；貌似真实但实际不可靠的
The scholar rejected the spurious quotation. spurious 常用于论据、文本、出处等不可靠的“假”。

反义词

genuine
真正的
The museum confirmed that the coin was genuine. genuine 是 counterfeit 最直接的反义。
authentic
真实可靠的；原作的
Experts verified that the painting was authentic. authentic 更强调来源可靠、不是仿冒。

词族 / 派生

派生词

counterfeiter
伪造者
The counterfeiter copied the bills with surprising care.

同根 / 迁移联想

counter-
相反；对抗；对应
A counterargument challenged the speaker's claim.

choreograph

/ˈkɔːriəɡræf/ · v. · Page 275

to plan movements or events carefully and deliberately

编舞；精心安排或设计

联想

choreography 是“舞蹈编排”；choreograph 在 GRE/正式语境中常引申为“精心安排一场活动或流程”。

意思、用法与例句

编排舞蹈或动作

设计舞蹈、表演或动作的顺序。

常见表达choreograph a dancechoreograph a scene

The director choreographed the fight scene so that every movement looked natural.

查看翻译和讲解

导演设计了打斗场景的动作，让每个动作看起来都自然。

这里是最直观的“编排动作”。

精心安排活动或流程

把会议、活动、宣传或政治场面设计得很有控制感。

常见表达choreograph a meetingchoreograph an eventcarefully choreographed

He carefully choreographed the meeting to avoid any open disagreement.

查看翻译和讲解

他精心安排了会议，以避免公开分歧。

原书例句中的 meeting 显示 choreograph 的引申义：不是随便安排，而是有意控制流程。

The ceremony was choreographed to project unity and confidence.

查看翻译和讲解

这场仪式被精心安排，以展示团结和信心。

正式语境中常暗示安排得很刻意。

同义 / 反义

近义词

arrange
安排
She arranged the speakers in a careful order. arrange 普通；choreograph 更强调设计每一步和整体效果。
plan
计划
The team planned the event months in advance. plan 是广义计划；choreograph 更像控制流程和呈现。
orchestrate
精心策划；协调安排
The campaign was orchestrated to attract maximum attention. orchestrate 和 choreograph 都有精心安排感；orchestrate 更常用于多方协调。

反义词

improvise
即兴处理
When the schedule collapsed, the speaker had to improvise. improvise 强调临场发挥，与精心编排相反。

词族 / 派生

派生词

choreography
编舞；动作或流程设计
The choreography made the battle scene feel precise.
choreographer
编舞者；动作设计者
The choreographer planned every movement on stage.

aggregate

/ˈæɡrɪɡət/ · adj./n./v. · Page 275

formed by combining many separate parts; a total amount

总计的；总数；合计

联想

total 是普通的“总数”；aggregate 更强调许多个分散部分合在一起得到的整体。

意思、用法与例句

数据、金额或数量的总和

把许多单独项目合并后得到的总体数值。

常见表达aggregate amountaggregate demandaggregate data

The aggregate amount of grants exceeded the committee's estimate.

查看翻译和讲解

拨款总额超过了委员会的估计。

aggregate amount 表示把多笔金额合在一起后的总额。

Aggregate data can hide large differences among individual groups.

查看翻译和讲解

汇总数据可能掩盖不同群体之间的巨大差异。

这里 aggregate 与 individual 形成对比：整体数据不等于个体差异。

作为动词表示合计或聚合

把分散的信息、数据或项目集中起来。

常见表达aggregate resultsaggregate information

The researchers aggregated results from several small studies.

查看翻译和讲解

研究人员汇总了几项小型研究的结果。

动词 aggregate 强调把分散结果合并分析。

同义 / 反义

近义词

total
总数；总计的
The total cost was higher than expected. total 最普通；aggregate 更强调由多个部分合成。
sum
总和
The sum of the payments was recorded at the end of the report. sum 更偏数学加总；aggregate 可用于统计和社会科学语境。
cumulative
累积的
The cumulative effect of small changes was significant. cumulative 强调随时间累积；aggregate 强调多个部分合在一起。

反义词

individual
单个的；个体的
Individual responses varied widely despite the high aggregate score. individual 与 aggregate 的整体视角相反。

词族 / 派生

派生词

aggregation
聚合；汇总
Data aggregation can hide important individual differences.
aggregated
被汇总的；聚合后的
Aggregated results can reveal a broader pattern.

同根 / 迁移联想

segregate
隔离；分开
The policy segregated students by ability.

demarcate

/ˈdiːmɑːrkeɪt/ · v. · Page 275

to mark or set the boundary or limits of something

划定界线；区分

联想

mark 是“标出”；demarcate 更正式，强调把界线、范围或类别划清楚。

意思、用法与例句

划定实际边界

标出土地、国家、区域等实际界线。

常见表达demarcate a boundarya border demarcated by a river

The border was demarcated by a river.

查看翻译和讲解

边界由一条河划定。

这是 demarcate 最直观的边界用法。

区分概念或范围

在理论、职责、类别或阶段之间划清界限。

常见表达demarcate the limitsdemarcate one category from another

The essay demarcates scientific explanation from moral judgment.

查看翻译和讲解

这篇文章区分了科学解释和道德判断。

GRE 阅读中常见这种抽象边界：概念之间要分清。

Clear rules help demarcate the responsibilities of each department.

查看翻译和讲解

明确的规则有助于划清各部门的职责。

demarcate responsibilities 表示把责任范围划定。

同义 / 反义

近义词

mark
标出
A row of stones marked the path. mark 很普通；demarcate 更正式，强调界线或限度。
distinguish
区分
The study distinguishes correlation from causation. distinguish 强调看出差别；demarcate 强调把界线划出来。
delineate
描绘；界定
The author delineates the main stages of the process. delineate 常用于清楚描述轮廓、范围或步骤。
demarcation
分界线；划分
The demarcation between public and private life became less clear. demarcation 是名词，表示界线或划分本身。

反义词

blur
模糊界线
The new technology blurs the line between work and leisure. blur the line 与 demarcate 的“划清界线”相反。

词族 / 派生

派生词

demarcation
分界线；划分
The river formed a clear demarcation between the two regions.

trade-off

/ˈtreɪd ɔːf/ · n. · Page 275

a balance between two desirable but competing things

权衡；取舍

联想

trade 是“交换”；trade-off 就像为了得到一件好处，必须放弃或牺牲另一件好处。

意思、用法与例句

在两个好处之间权衡

讨论风险与收益、速度与质量、自由与安全等不能兼得的选择。

常见表达a trade-off between A and Bmake a trade-offrisk-benefit trade-off

The policy involves a trade-off between privacy and convenience.

查看翻译和讲解

这项政策涉及隐私和便利之间的取舍。

a trade-off between A and B 是最核心结构。

Researchers must consider the trade-off between speed and accuracy.

查看翻译和讲解

研究人员必须考虑速度和准确性之间的权衡。

GRE/学术语境中常讨论两种价值之间的冲突。

同义 / 反义

近义词

compromise
折中；妥协
The final design was a compromise between cost and durability. compromise 强调折中方案；trade-off 强调得到一方就要牺牲另一方。
balance
平衡
The plan tries to balance efficiency with fairness. balance 可作动词；trade-off 是取舍关系本身。
concession
让步
The agreement required concessions from both sides. concession 强调让出某些要求；trade-off 不一定涉及谈判。
exchange
交换
The exchange of information helped both teams. exchange 是普通交换；trade-off 强调互相冲突的价值之间取舍。

反义词

win-win
双赢
The proposal was presented as a win-win solution for both groups. win-win 表示双方都受益；trade-off 表示存在牺牲。

查看翻译和讲解

这幅画使用暗沉的颜色来营造失落感。

gloomy

sober
清醒的；严肃的

反义词

The cheerful music changed the mood of the room. cheerful 与 somber 的沉重感相反。

perfunctory

/pərˈfʌŋktəri/ · adj. · Page 275

done quickly or routinely, without real care or interest

敷衍的；草率的

联想

意思、用法与例句

动作或回应只是走过场

做了某个动作，但明显不认真、不深入或缺少真实情感。

常见表达a perfunctory smilea perfunctory apologya perfunctory search

He gave a perfunctory smile and quickly turned back to his work.

查看翻译和讲解

他敷衍地笑了一下，很快又转回去工作。

perfunctory smile 表示笑得礼貌但没有真情绪。

The inspectors made only a perfunctory search of the building.

查看翻译和讲解

检查人员只是草草搜查了这栋楼。

perfunctory search 强调搜查流于形式、不细致。

评论或处理缺少真正投入

用于批评某种答复、审查或讨论太浅、太机械。

常见表达a perfunctory reviewa perfunctory answer

The committee's review was perfunctory, ignoring the most serious objections.

查看翻译和讲解

委员会的审查很敷衍，忽视了最严重的反对意见。

正式语境中常用来批评程序上做了、实质上没认真做。

同义 / 反义

近义词

careless
粗心的

casual
随意的；不正式的
His casual remark upset the audience. casual 可中性；perfunctory 多带批评，表示不够认真。
cursory
草率的；粗略的
The report gave the issue only a cursory glance. cursory 强调看得很粗略；perfunctory 强调态度敷衍。
superficial
肤浅的；表面的
The analysis was superficial and missed the deeper cause. superficial 强调停留表面；perfunctory 强调完成动作但不用心。

反义词

thorough
彻底的；细致的
The committee conducted a thorough investigation. thorough 与 perfunctory 的草率相反。
earnest
认真的；真诚的

词族 / 派生

/ʌnˈflɪntʃɪŋ/ · adj. · Page 276

not turning away from difficulty, danger, or unpleasant truth

不退缩的；直面现实的

联想

The book offers an unflinching account of political corruption.

这本书毫不回避地描述了政治腐败。

unflinching account 常用于严肃报道或评论。

坚定不退缩

形容人在压力、批评或危险面前不动摇。

常见表达unflinching supportunflinching resolve

She showed unflinching resolve during the crisis.

查看翻译和讲解

危机中她表现出坚定不退缩的决心。

这里强调面对压力时不畏缩。

同义 / 反义

近义词

resolute
坚决的
