yield

/jiːld/ · v.; n. · Page 288

to produce a result, give way to pressure, or refer to an amount produced

产生；带来；让步；产量

联想

produce 是“产生”；yield 更常用于方法、过程或土地“产出结果”，也可表示人在压力下让步。

意思、用法与例句

方法或过程产生结果

某个方法、行动或过程带来结果、收益或数据。

常见表达yield resultsyield evidenceyield a profit

This method yields the same results in repeated trials.

查看翻译和讲解

这一方法在重复试验中产生相同结果。

yield results 是这个用法。

The survey yielded little evidence of public support.

查看翻译和讲解

调查几乎没有产生公众支持的证据。

学术语境里 yield evidence 很自然。

在压力或要求下让步

面对论点、要求或压力时退让。

常见表达yield to pressureyield to demandsrefuse to yield

The company refused to yield to the protesters' demands.

查看翻译和讲解

公司拒绝向抗议者的要求让步。

yield to demands 是让步义。

土地或产业的产量

农作物、工业或投资产生的数量。

常见表达crop yieldyield of wheathigh yield

The new seed increased the yield of wheat per acre.

查看翻译和讲解

新品种提高了每英亩小麦产量。

yield 作名词表示产量。

同义 / 反义

近义词

produce
产生
The process produced reliable results. produce 最普通；yield 更常用于结果、数据、收益自然产出。
give in
让步
The mayor refused to give in to pressure. give in 更口语；yield 更正式。
engender
引发；产生
The policy engendered public distrust. engender 强调引发某种状态或情绪。
succumb
屈服；抵挡不住
The government succumbed to public pressure. succumb 更强调被压力压倒。
output
产出；产量
Factory output rose in June. output 是产出量；yield 可指产量，也可作动词。

反义词

resist
抵抗；不让步
The group resisted pressure to change its position. resist 与 yield to pressure 相反。
withhold
保留；不交出
The agency withheld the data. withhold 与 yield/produce information 的交出或产出相反。

词族 / 派生

派生词

yielding
柔顺的；易弯曲的
The yielding surface reduced the force of the fall.
unyielding
不让步的；坚硬的
Her unyielding stance frustrated the negotiators.

同根 / 迁移联想

output
产出；产量
The factory increased its output this year.

strongly disliking or opposed to something

联想

意思、用法与例句

对某事反感或不愿接受

常见表达be averse to riskbe averse to flatterynot averse to change

He is averse to flattery and distrusts excessive praise.

他反感奉承，也不信任过度赞美。

be averse to flattery 对应原书。

Investors who are averse to risk may prefer safer assets.

查看翻译和讲解

厌恶风险的投资者可能更喜欢安全资产。

risk-averse 是非常常见的相关表达。

同义 / 反义

adverse

shy

/ʃaɪ/ · v.; adj. · Page 288

to avoid something because of fear or dislike; also timid or reserved

回避；躲开；害羞的

联想

avoid 是“避开”；shy away from 更像因为害怕、紧张或不喜欢而退缩。

意思、用法与例句

因为紧张或不愿意而回避

面对公众、风险、责任或困难话题时退缩、避开。

常见表达shy away from publicityshy away from riskshy from controversy

The author shies away from publicity and rarely gives interviews.

查看翻译和讲解

这位作家回避媒体关注，很少接受采访。

shy away from publicity 对应原书。

The committee should not shy away from difficult questions.

查看翻译和讲解

委员会不应回避困难问题。

常用于抽象争议或责任。

人害羞、拘谨

性格上不善于和人接触，容易紧张。

常见表达a shy childshy around strangers

He was shy around strangers but confident with close friends.

查看翻译和讲解

他在陌生人面前害羞，但和密友在一起很自信。

形容词义是基础义。

同义 / 反义

近义词

avoid
避免；避开
The speaker avoided the most controversial issue. avoid 最普通；shy away from 带退缩或不愿面对的感觉。
timid
胆怯的；羞怯的
The timid child spoke softly. timid 对应形容词 shy 的性格义。
evade
回避；逃避
The official evaded the question. evade 常暗示有意逃避责任或问题。
recoil
退缩；畏缩
She recoiled from the idea of public debate. recoil 更强，带身体或心理后退感。
short of
差一点；不足
The total was just shy of one million dollars. just shy of 是“差一点到”，不是害羞或回避。

反义词

confront
面对；正视
The report confronts the problem directly. confront 与 shy away from 相反。
outgoing
外向的
Her outgoing personality made interviews easy. outgoing 与形容词 shy 相反。

词族 / 派生

派生词

shyness
害羞；羞怯
Her shyness made the first interview difficult.
shying
回避中的；躲避
The company kept shying away from public questions.

同根 / 迁移联想

avoidance
回避

invaluable

/ɪnˈvæljuəbəl/ · adj. · Page 288

extremely useful or valuable

极有价值的；非常宝贵的

联想

valuable 是“有价值的”；invaluable 不是“没有价值”，而是“价值大到难以估量”。

意思、用法与例句

帮助、建议或资源极其有用

某物非常有帮助、价值难以估量。

常见表达invaluable helpinvaluable advicean invaluable resource

Thank you for your invaluable help during the project.

查看翻译和讲解

谢谢你在项目中提供的宝贵帮助。

invaluable help 对应原书。

Her experience proved invaluable when the team faced a crisis.

查看翻译和讲解

团队遇到危机时，她的经验证明极其宝贵。

prove invaluable 是自然结构。

健康、机会等珍贵到难以用钱衡量

强调某种东西非常珍贵，不只是价格高。

常见表达an invaluable blessinginvaluable experience

Good health is an invaluable blessing.

查看翻译和讲解

健康是千金难买的福气。

对应

同义 / 反义

近义词

valuable
有价值的
The advice was valuable to new teachers. valuable 表示有价值；invaluable 表示价值高到难以估量。
useful
有用的
The guide was useful during the trip. useful 只说有用；invaluable 强调非常有用。
indispensable
不可或缺的
Accurate data are indispensable to the study. indispensable 强调少了不行。
priceless
无价的；极珍贵的
The letter is a priceless record of the period. priceless 更偏珍贵或情感价值。
valueless
没有价值的
The rumor was valueless to the investigation. invaluable 容易误解成“无价值”，但实际是“极有价值”。

反义词

worthless
无价值的
The forged document was worthless as evidence. worthless 与 invaluable 的高价值相反。
useless
无用的
The broken tool was useless. useless 与 extremely useful 相反。

词族 / 派生

in- 在 invaluable 中不是普通否定，而是加强为“价值难以衡量”。

派生词

value
价值；重视
The archive has great historical value.
valuation
估价；评价
The valuation of the property took several days.

disown

/dɪsˈoʊn/ · v. · Page 288

to refuse to acknowledge a connection with someone or something

否认与……有关；断绝关系

联想

own 是“承认是自己的”；disown 就是“不认”，可以是否认话、行为、责任，也可以和人断绝关系。

意思、用法与例句

否认某话、行为或责任和自己有关

拒绝承认某个说法、行为、责任出自自己。

常见表达disown a statementdisown responsibilitydisown any hand in something

The minister quickly disowned the statement after it caused public anger.

查看翻译和讲解

这句话引发公众愤怒后，部长很快否认它代表自己。

He disowned any hand in the book.

查看翻译和讲解

他否认曾参与这本书的编写。

disown any hand in something 是原书结构。

和某人断绝关系

正式或情感上不再承认某人为家庭、团体或阵营的一员。

常见表达disown a childdisown a former ally

The family disowned him after the scandal.

查看翻译和讲解

丑闻之后，家人和他断绝关系。

人际关系中 disown 语气很重。

同义 / 反义

近义词

deny
否认
She denied making the comment. deny 更普通；disown 强调不承认关系或责任。
reject
拒绝；不接受
The committee rejected the proposal. reject 是拒绝接受；disown 是不认作自己的。
repudiate
拒绝承认；正式否认
The leader repudiated the agreement. repudiate 更正式，常用于原则、协议或责任。
renounce
声明放弃；断绝
He renounced his claim to the property. renounce 强调主动宣布放弃。
disavow
否认；不承认
The organization disavowed the violent statement. disavow 与 disown 接近，更正式，常用于公开声明。

反义词

acknowledge
承认
The author acknowledged his role in the project. acknowledge 与 disown 直接相反。
claim
声称拥有；认领
Several groups claimed responsibility for the attack. claim responsibility 与 disown responsibility 相反。

词族 / 派生

dis- 表示分离或否定；own 是“拥有/承认属于自己”。

派生词

disowned
被断绝关系的；被否认的
The disowned statement disappeared from the website.

同根 / 迁移联想

disavow
否认；不承认
The party disavowed the candidate's remarks.

genealogy

/ˌdʒiːniˈælədʒi/ · n. · Page 288

a line of family descent, or the study of that descent

家谱；血统；谱系

联想

family tree 是“家谱”；genealogy 更正式，可以指家族血统的研究，也可比喻思想、概念的来源谱系。

意思、用法与例句

家族血统和家谱研究

追溯一个家族从祖先延续下来的血统线。

常见表达trace a genealogyresearch one's genealogyfamily genealogy

They have been researching their genealogies for years.

查看翻译和讲解

他们多年来一直在研究自己的家谱。

对应

The archive helped her trace her genealogy back three centuries.

查看翻译和讲解

档案帮助她把家谱追溯到三百年前。

trace genealogy 是自然结构。

观念、制度或传统的来源谱系

在学术写作中，比喻追溯一个观念、制度或文化形式的历史来源。

常见表达the genealogy of an ideaintellectual genealogycultural genealogy

The book traces the genealogy of modern environmental thought.

查看翻译和讲解

这本书追溯了现代环保思想的谱系。

学术语境中 genealogy 可比喻思想来源。

同义 / 反义

近义词

family tree
家谱
The family tree shows four generations. family tree 更日常、图像化；genealogy 更正式。
lineage
血统；世系
The family claims a royal lineage. lineage 强调一条血统线。
ancestry
祖先；血统
She studied her ancestry through old records. ancestry 更强调祖先来源。
descent
血统；传承
The tradition is passed down through descent. descent 是从祖先延续下来的线。
genetics
遗传学
Genetics explains how traits are inherited. genetics 是生物遗传机制；genealogy 是家族谱系或来源追溯。

词族 / 派生

genea- 与 birth/family lineage 有关；-logy 表示研究。

派生词

genealogical
家谱的；谱系的
Genealogical records helped identify the family.
genealogist
家谱学者；家谱研究者
A genealogist found the missing branch of the family.

同根 / 迁移联想

lineage
血统；世系
The lineage can be traced through written records.

联想

political gaffe 很常见。

Forgetting the host's name was an unforgivable social gaffe.

查看翻译和讲解

忘记主人的名字是一次不可原谅的社交失礼。

同义 / 反义

近义词

mistake
错误
The error was a simple mistake. mistake 范围很宽；gaffe 特指尴尬的社交或公开失误。
blunder

faux pas
失礼；失态

indiscretion
轻率失言；不慎行为
The letter revealed a youthful indiscretion. indiscretion 强调轻率或判断失误。
gaff
鱼叉；钩杆
The fisherman used a gaff to lift the fish. gaff 是工具；gaffe 是失言/失礼。

反义词

tact
得体；圆滑
She handled the question with tact. tact 是避免 gaffe 的社交分寸。

词族 / 派生

派生词

gaffe-prone
容易失言的

/ˌɪl ədˈvaɪzd/ · adj. · Page 289

not sensible because it was done without enough thought or good advice

不明智的；欠考虑的

联想

unwise 是“不明智”；ill-advised 更像一个决定或行动在做之前就缺少充分考虑。

意思、用法与例句

决定、行动或评论欠考虑

行动前缺少足够判断、建议或审慎思考，因此显得不明智。

常见表达an ill-advised decisionan ill-advised moveill-advised remarks

The mayor made an ill-advised decision to ignore the warnings.

市长无视警告的决定很不明智。

His ill-advised remarks damaged the company's reputation.

查看翻译和讲解

他欠考虑的言论损害了公司的声誉。

常用于公开言论。

同义 / 反义

近义词

unwise
不明智的
It was unwise to ignore the evidence. unwise 最普通；ill-advised 强调缺少考虑或建议。
foolish
愚蠢的；不明智的
The plan seemed foolish from the start. foolish 语气更直接、更口语。
imprudent
不审慎的
It was imprudent to invest all the funds at once. imprudent 更正式，强调缺少谨慎。
misguided
误导的；判断错误的
The reform was based on a misguided assumption. misguided 强调方向或判断被错误想法带偏。
ill-mannered
没礼貌的
The ill-mannered guest interrupted everyone. ill-mannered 是礼貌问题；ill-advised 是判断问题。

反义词

prudent
审慎的
A prudent investor keeps some money in reserve. prudent 与 ill-advised 直接相反。
well-advised
明智的；考虑周全的
The delay was a well-advised precaution. well-advised 是结构上的直接对照。

词族 / 派生

ill- 在复合词中表示“不好、糟糕”；advised 来自 advice。

派生词

advice
建议
Good advice can prevent an ill-advised choice.
advisable
可取的；明智的
It is advisable to read the contract carefully.
well-advised
明智的；考虑周全的
Her cautious response was well-advised.

to become smaller, catch a disease, or make a formal agreement

查看翻译和讲解

危机期间经济萎缩。

感染疾病

人染上病毒、疾病或感染。

常见表达contract a viruscontract a diseasecontract an infection

Several workers contracted the virus during the outbreak.

查看翻译和讲解

疫情期间几名工人感染了病毒。

contract a virus 对应原书。

订立合同或协议

正式达成合同关系。

常见表达contract with a companycontract to do work

查看翻译和讲解

dwindle

constrict

反义词

dilate
扩张；扩大

词族 / 派生

收紧；压缩
The bandage can constrict blood flow.

substantial legacy 对应原书。

过去留下的影响或后果

人、政策、战争或时代留下的长期影响，可以正面也可以负面。

常见表达a lasting legacythe legacy of warleave a legacylegacy system

The leader left a mixed legacy of reform and repression.
